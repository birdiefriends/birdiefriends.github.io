## Session BP-1 · June 18, 2026

**Focus:** Initial vision free-form, Gate 1 development, capability inventory creation

**Key decisions:**
- Gathering design platform framing established as the core vision (scoring = infrastructure, recommendation engine = product, memorialization = what's paid for)
- Customer hierarchy settled: organizer leads, participant is enrollment pipeline, Ambassador is accelerant not dependency
- "Entrepreneur" rejected in favor of **Ambassador** — community advocate framing, not sales channel
- Franchise model preferred over MLM — one-level residual only, explicit rejection of multi-level structure
- Legal gambling risk identified and resolved: platform fee structurally decoupled from any prize pool; "VIG" terminology abandoned
- BF Pay (pot management) confirmed as high-value but legally gated — Phase 2, not launch-blocking, but legal review moved earlier in priority sequence than originally assumed
- BF Rewards (physical/digital achievement recognition) confirmed as **core**, not optional — validated by Garrett's Last Swing hat as organic pre-commercial proof point
- Gathering spectrum identified — serious golf groups vs. social/mixed trips vs. bucket-list vs. corporate/charity are distinct products on shared infrastructure
- Napkin break-even calculated: ~3-5 events/month covers commercial fixed costs, achievable without scale
- Cross-community/cross-region competition identified as a future network-effect layer once Ambassador communities exist
- Founder context clarified: this is a retirement contribution project with low-investment, high-optionality framing — not a venture growth play. Explicitly not ambitious in the VC sense.
- Tone principle established: empathy over edge when describing who BF is not for (e.g., "cheap golfers aren't our customer" revised to fit-based, non-judgmental framing)

Treated as a deliberate scaling/moat mechanism. Permanence-vs-expiration mechanics explicitly flagged as open and unresolved — to be modeled carefully at Gate 4, not assumed.
- Tone correction applied: "cheap golfers aren't our customer" revised to empathetic, fit-based framing rather than character judgment — now a standing tone principle in the Vision doc.
