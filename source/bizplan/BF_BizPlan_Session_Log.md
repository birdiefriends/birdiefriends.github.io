## Session BP-4 · July 2, 2026

**Focus:** Replace per-player lifetime attribution with a Community Remit Share model; define the full Ambassador "remit" (all payable BF services, not just round fees); resolve dues billing/commission recognition mechanics; lock the 50% share as a deliberate philosophical stance

**Key decisions:**
- **Community Remit Share adopted, superseding BP-3's per-player mechanic.** Root cause identified: a per-player 5% recurring override can't clear a meaningful monthly income target at any %, because a single home-course community's attributable revenue pool is too small. New mechanic: an Ambassador who seeds a community earns **50% of that community's total remit** (round fees + package upsells + rewards margin + recognized dues), not just personally-originated players. Founder's stated target — $1,000/month, "a real and helpful number that helps pay for a golf habit" — used as the design bar throughout.
- **Origination kept, stacked on top.** The old 20% one-time origination commission remains as an instant per-player bonus, layered on the ongoing remit share. Modeled as financially near-negligible ($4–12/month per community) but recruiting-narrative-positive.
- **Remit services explicitly defined** (see Gate Log Gate 2): round/event platform fee, event package upsells, BF Rewards fulfillment margin, BF Pay settlement fees (Phase 2), and a new community/season dues component.
- **Key structural finding: cadence matters as much as % or base.** $1,000/month is only reachable from a single community when it runs near-weekly (BF Series-style), not occasional Gatherings — an infrequent community can't get there at any share rate. Logged as a strategic implication for Ambassador recruiting/coaching materials.
- **Subscription question narrowly reopened as "community/season dues," then refined into tiered membership.** Distinguished explicitly from the general SaaS-subscription skepticism already settled in Vision — framed as golf-club-style seasonal dues, not a recurring app subscription. **Refined mid-session from a flat per-player dues fee into three tiers:** BF Member (free — non-competitive Gatherings), Competitive Member ($2/mo — required for BF Series), Host ($1/mo — hosting privilege, any tier). Competitive and Host are independent, mutually-exclusive products with separate billing/recognition, not merged. Better aligned with empathy-over-edge tone (nobody pays just to show up and play) and the buyer-segmentation table already in Gate 2.
- **Host tier includes a trial: 2 free lifetime Host credits, then subscription required.** Deliberately lifetime (not annual reset) to preserve the fee's function as a genuine commitment test, while still lowering the barrier for someone to try hosting once or twice — balances the growth thesis (more attempted Gatherings) against the Host fee's role as a binary "is this a real Host" signal. Considered and rejected: bundling free hosting into the Competitive tier, which would have diluted that same signal. Founder explicitly accepted the minor downside (occasional one-month-subscribe-then-cancel) as worth the growth upside.
- **Dues billing/recognition mechanic settled: Path B — annual charge, monthly-recognized commission.** Each membership tier (Competitive $24/yr, Host $12/yr) is billed once annually and recognized 1/12 monthly; Ambassador is paid only on recognized (consumed) months. Mid-year cancellation refunds only unrecognized future months — Ambassador commission already paid on elapsed months is never clawed back, by construction. Chosen over straight monthly billing (simpler for BF cash flow, same commission smoothness) and over a short-refund-window lump-sum model (loses the monthly smoothing).
- **50% share locked in as a deliberate philosophical stance, not just a calibrated number.** Founder's framing: build helpful tech, share the profit generously with the people doing the community work, run BF itself on thinner margins, make it up on volume and growth. Logged as new Vision Core Thesis 10. BF's own break-even bar stays low enough (napkin: ~5 events/month per community, or ~2 medium communities) that this costs founder upside, not business viability.
- **Competitor spot-check performed** (ParUp Golf, Squabbit, Golf GameBook, Golf Genius, Unknown Golf, 18Birdies) — confirms Thesis 1: all tech, no paired business model or human distribution layer. Logged as Gate 3 validation.
- **New technical dependency identified:** a dues ledger (per-player monthly recognition schedule) is a new, small D1 schema build item — not yet scoped as a dev-session task, logged in Capability Inventory roadmap and flagged as a Gate 6 launch-readiness item.
