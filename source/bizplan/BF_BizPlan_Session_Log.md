## Session BP-1 · June 18, 2026

**Focus:** Initial vision free-form, Gate 1 development, capability inventory creation

**Key decisions:**
- Gathering design platform framing established as the core vision (scoring = infrastructure, recommendation engine = product, memorialization = what's paid for)
- Customer hierarchy settled: organizer leads, participant is enrollment pipeline, Ambassador is accelerant not dependency
- "Entrepreneur" rejected in favor of **Ambassador** — community advocate framing, not sales channel
- Franchise model preferred over MLM — one-level residual only, explicit rejection of multi-level structure
- Legal gambling risk identified and resolved: platform fee structurally decoupled from any prize pool; "VIG" terminology abandoned
- BF Pay (pot management) confirmed as high-value but legally gated — Phase 2, not launch-blocking, but legal review moved earlier in priority sequence than originally assumed
- BF Rewards (physical/digital achievement recognition) confirmed as **core**, not optional — validated by Garrett's Last Swing hat as organic pre-commercial proof point
- Gathering spectrum identified — serious golf groups vs. social/mixed trips vs. bucket-list vs. corporate/charity are distinct products on shared infrastructure
- Napkin break-even calculated: ~3-5 events/month covers commercial fixed costs, achievable without scale
- Cross-community/cross-region competition identified as a future network-effect layer once Ambassador communities exist
- Founder context clarified: this is a retirement contribution project with low-investment, high-optionality framing — not a venture growth play. Explicitly not ambitious in the VC sense.
- Tone principle established: empathy over edge when describing who BF is not for (e.g., "cheap golfers aren't our customer" revised to fit-based, non-judgmental framing)

**Artifacts created:**

## Session BP-2 · June 19, 2026

**Focus:** Tidy Gate 1 to close; open Gate 2 buyer segmentation and entry-vector model

**Key decisions:**
- Gate 1 confirmed ready to close at the direction/tone/vision level — founder confirmed no new thinking emerged since BP-1 that changes the big-picture frame
- Pricing **philosophy** settled (low per-unit, volume + repeatability + add-on driven) — distinct from the pricing **number**, which stays open and parked at Gate 4
- "Cockroach wrangling" language retired from all bizplan docs — entertaining internally but wrong tone externally; replaced with neutral "group-organizing" language (Vision, Gate Log Gate 1, Capability Inventory all updated)
- Outdated "cheap golfers aren't our customer" quote in Capability Inventory updated to match the empathetic tone principle already established in Vision
- Buyer segmentation reframed twice during the session: first attempt (single funnel: free hook → four monetization layers) was correctly rejected by founder as not actually true; second attempt (population segments × roles, two axes not one list) was validated by founder's own isolated-golfer/$MG anecdote and adopted
- **Multi-vector entry model settled:** free organizer hook, direct-to-player marketing, and Ambassador-led community capture are three independent, non-exclusive on-ramps, not stages of one funnel — cross-exposure between vectors (not the entry vector itself) is the real stickiness mechanism. Vector prioritization explicitly deferred to Gate 5, not decided at Gate 2.
- Money Group ($MG) tested as a possible standalone population segment, rejected — it's a role (stakes/pot runner) that occurs inside Trips and League populations, not its own population. Ambassador confirmed as the same kind of cross-cutting role.
- **New Gate 3 proof point added:** founder's own customer-discovery anecdote — GS formalizes in software what a manual $MG was already doing on paper — gives BF a second independent real-world validation pattern (alongside the GLS hat) that it productizes existing human behavior rather than inventing new behavior
- New "isolated golfer" persona captured as a Gate 1 customer-discovery thread in Vision doc — broadens customer understanding beyond "the organizer" to include the participant who gets socially found and pulled in
- **New Vision Core Thesis 9 added:** BF's responsibility is infrastructure and barrier removal, not predicting or prescribing who plays what role. Founder challenged the segmentation work itself mid-session with real counter-examples (a league commissioner who dislikes the role but honors his late father's legacy; the founder's own unplanned path into running BF; a possible future case of someone stepping in for an injured Wrangler; someone finding an Ambassador slot as unplanned income opportunity) — motivation is unpredictable and BF shouldn't need to sort by it. This reframed the segmentation tables from "the four types of BF user" into explicitly illustrative planning archetypes, not a taxonomy imposed on users.
- Population × role segmentation formalized into clean tables in Gate Log (was prose-only before), with explicit framing as illustrative, non-prescriptive
- **New capability gap surfaced:** role succession/handoff (someone stepping into a vacated Wrangler/Organizer/Ambassador role) is not currently a named platform capability — added to Capability Inventory roadmap
- **BF Pay legal review sequencing settled:** gated on an explicit founder commercialization commitment, not cost/time/volume. Founder is genuinely undecided whether to commercialize at all; platform development continues regardless as an intentional disconnect. Reframed the plan itself as part of how that decision gets made, not documentation of a decision already made — captured as a standing note in Vision's Founder Context section so future sessions don't assume plan completion implies commercialization intent.
- **Gate 2's "6–12 months" timeline framing corrected:** founder identified the original timeline as a reflexive, emotional answer (avoiding an open-ended multi-year process) rather than a deliberate constraint — not yet invested in any timeline at this feasibility/planning stage. Gate 2's framework question softened to drop the hard timeline. Founder also named the actual staged process expected going forward: (1) intellectual conclusion on whether something worthy exists, (2) if yes, possible paths to making it real, (3) commitment questions, (4) risk-profile debate — explicitly flagged as the stage that gets "interesting." Captured as a standing process note in the Gate Log framework section.
- **Two execution-capacity risks identified, distinct from model soundness:** (1) architecture/dev-at-scale gap — the current platform is a validated prototype, not built for what Gates 1–2 now describe (multi-tenant, payments, automated ingestion, recommendation engine, BF Pay compliance); unclear who closes this gap. (2) Community sweat-equity catalyst gap — the Ambassador model assumes Ambassadors emerge once the product exists, but the network has a real cold-start problem requiring an unglamorous hands-on first mover; GLS proves the founder can do this once, not that it scales as a repeated growth engine. Both added as new Cross-Gate Risks (affecting Gates 2, 4, 5, 6), explicitly separated from the founder's personal financial investment decisions, which remain outside this plan's scope.
- **Multi-tenant architecture gap has an active mitigation in motion:** founder is starting a parallel dev experiment specifically on multi-tenant architecture, validated by at least 3 BFs who have expressed direct interest — not hypothetical demand. Previously infeasible under the old Jotform single-tenant data model; no longer blocked. Tagline **"Tee off, play great"** introduced and tied directly to this capability — the promise only scales if communities can stand up their own instance without the founder hand-operating each one. Architecture/dev-at-scale risk row updated to reflect this is partially in motion, not fully unaddressed.
- **Tee-time reservation gap identified, then corrected (known, longstanding, now formally logged):** BF is explicitly not in the tee-time reservation business — every course runs its own system. Initially logged as both a barrier and opportunity; founder corrected this — it's not actually a funnel barrier, since every future multi-tenant user will already interact with a golf club to launch a BF event (same pattern as founder/BSGC, and as Matt independently scheduling/launching GLS with BF only capturing it after). The real open item is a *future* automation/partnership feature (some reservation systems offer fee-based APIs), not a current gap — parked accordingly.
- **GHIN handicap integration flagged as the same pattern:** official GHIN access costs ~$6k/yr — an economic barrier today (BF currently calculates handicaps internally), but possibly not at scale. Logged as a future economics question, not a launch blocker, alongside the tee-time API item.
